Clearing the Air

Sulfur Dioxide Levels in the Top 15 Most Polluted Cities (1990-2014)

Sulfur dioxide is a reactive gas emitted by power plants from the process of fossil fuel combustion (coal-fire). Standards for SO2 were first set by the EPA in 1971. Standards were revised in 2010. The new 1-hour standard is 75 parts per billion. The symbols outlined in orange represent cities above this standard.

Cities on this map are the most polluted by year-round particle pollution, as determined by the American Lung Association's 2015 State of the Air. Sulfur dioxide is one component of particular pollution.

The coal-fired power plants with output over 2,000 MW are displayed. Coal-fired power plants show a strong correlation with SO2 pollution.
