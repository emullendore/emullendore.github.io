Internet Freedom and Censorship

// an analysis of access, political rights, civil liberties, and internet filtering around the world

As defined by the U.S. Department of State, internet freedom ensures that "any child, born anywhere in the world, has access to the global Internet as an open platform on which to innovate, learn, organize, and express herself free from undue interference or censorship." Yet, this right is restricted everywhere around the world under the direction of authoritarian leaders. As you explore the map and chart, you'll see that the extent of censorship and access varies greatly by nation.

// Internet Users per 100 People and Average Connection Speed

As connectivity grows at an ever-increasing rate, the importance of reliable internet access for all has become vital. Countries with lower internet access access can miss out on opportunies for furthering education, increasing productivity, and building communities over the web.[1][2]

// Freedom House Ratings for Political Rights and Civil Liberties

The organization Freedom House examines the freedoms and rights enjoyed by citizens around the world. Political rights are assessed by a country's electoral process, political participation, and how effective the government fucntions. Civil liberties look into freedom of expression, right to organize, as well as personal autonomy. On a scale of 1-7, 1 = most free and 7 = least free. [3][4]
